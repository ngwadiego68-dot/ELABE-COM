Using the ELABE Academic Portal effectively can significantly boost your performance in the GCE Ordinary and Advanced Levels. Follow this three-step methodology to master your subjects:

1. Master the Concepts (Use Subject Notes)

  1. Identify Topics: Navigate to your subject (e.g., Physics). Start with the topics you find most challenging (like Thermo or Mechanics).
  2. Read Definitions: Begin by reading the clear **definitions, units, and formulas** provided in the notes (e.g., on `physics-density.html`).
  3. Relate to the World: Pay attention to the **"Applications and Importance"** section to connect abstract ideas to real-life situations. This deepens understanding and memory.
  4. Verify with Search: Use the **Definition Search Bar** on the main page to quickly look up any unfamiliar technical terms you encounter in the notes.

2. Test Your Knowledge (Use Past Papers)

After reviewing the notes for a topic, move immediately to practice:

  1. Access the GCE Repository: Go to the **GCE Papers** section of the menu.
  2. Targeted Practice: Find past papers relevant to the subject you just studied. Try to solve questions related specifically to that topic (e.g., solving Pressure problems after reading the Pressure notes).
  3. Time Yourself: Treat your practice sessions like the real exam. This builds speed and stamina, crucial for GCE success.

3. Refine and Iterate (The Success Loop)

The key to scoring high is repetition and focused review:

  1. Analyze Mistakes: Identify the specific questions you got wrong in the past papers.
  2. Review Notes: Go back to the **ELABE notes** for those exact concepts. Re-read the relevant definition, formula, and example. Did you forget the unit? Did you use the wrong formula?
  3. Try Again: Attempt similar problems from other past papers. Don't move on until you consistently score well on that particular topic.

Success in the GCE is not about reading everything once; it's about reviewing the difficult points multiple times until they become second nature.
