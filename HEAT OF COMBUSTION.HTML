Defintion

This is heat change when one mole of a substance is completely burned in air (o2) e.g CH4 + 2O2 →CO2 + 2H2O

Determination of heat combustion of ethanol

Requirement:
  • ethanol
  • thin wall can
  • thermomether
  • distilled water
  • 100cm measuring cylinder
  • wind shield
  • spirit lamp
  • chemical balance
  • clamp and stand
    • procedure:
  • The spirit lamp is half filled with ethanol and tighly closed since ethanol is highly volatile
  • The spirit lamp and ethanol are weighed and the mass recorded
  • Exactly 100cm of distilled water is measured using the measuring cylinder and poured into the clamped thin walled can
  • The initial temperature of water is recorded .
  • The spirit lamp is lit and imediately placed under the metal can such that much of the flame is in contact with the metal can.
  • The flame is shielded from air current using the wind shield.
  • The water is stirred with the thermomether to ensure that the the heat is uniformly distributed while observing the temperature rise
  • When ther is a reasonable ries in temperature of about 20°, the final temperature is taken and the flame is immediately put off
  • The alcohol burner and content is then reweighed and it final mass taken
    • Calculation:

  • Heat absorbe by water= the heat evolveed by ethanol
  • Heat evolveed( ∆H) = Mg × C × ∆t
  • number of moles of ethanol=n

    • Heat of combustion=(mg × C × ∆t)/n
