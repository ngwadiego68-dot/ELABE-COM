1. The Atomic Structure and Nuclear Energy

Atomic Structure Review

  • Nucleus: Contains protons (positive charge) and neutrons (no charge).
  • Electrons: Orbit the nucleus in shells (negative charge).
  • Atomic Number (Z): Number of protons. Defines the element.
  • Mass Number (A): Total number of protons and neutrons.
  • Isotopes: Atoms of the same element (same Z) with different mass numbers (different number of neutrons).

Radioactivity

Radioactivity is the spontaneous decay of an unstable atomic nucleus by emitting radiation (alpha, beta, or gamma) to become more stable.

Type of Radiation Charge Nature Penetration Ionizing Power

Half-Life (1/2)

The time taken for half of the radioactive nuclei in a sample to decay (or for the activity to fall by half).

If the initial mass is $M_0$ and the number of half-lives is $n$, the remaining mass $M$ is:

$$M = M_0 \times \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^n$$

Uses of Radioactivity: Sterilization of equipment, Carbon dating, Smoke alarms ($\alpha$ source), Medical imaging and therapy.

Nuclear Fission: The splitting of a heavy nucleus into two smaller nuclei, releasing a large amount of energy (used in nuclear power plants).

2. Basic Electronics

The Cathode Ray Oscilloscope (C.R.O)

The C.R.O. is a device used to visually display and measure electrical signals (voltages). It plots the voltage on the Y-axis against time on the X-axis.

  • **Y-Gain Control:** Controls the vertical scale (**Voltage/Division**). Used to measure the amplitude (peak voltage).
  • **Time-Base Control:** Controls the horizontal scale (**Time/Division**). Used to measure the period of the wave.

Measuring A.C. Signals with the C.R.O

To measure an Alternating Current (A.C.) signal:

  1. **Peak Voltage ($\mathbf{V_p}$):** $V_p = (\text{Peak deflection on Y-axis}) \times (\text{Y-Gain setting})$
  2. **Period ($\mathbf{T}$):** $T = (\text{Length of one complete wave on X-axis}) \times (\text{Time-Base setting})$
  3. **Frequency ($\mathbf{f}$):** $f = \frac{1}{T}$

Logic Gates (Introduction)

Logic gates are electronic circuits that perform basic logical operations. They take one or more inputs and produce a single output (usually 1 or 0).

Gate Operation Symbol Truth Table (Example)
**AND** Output is 1 ONLY if **ALL** inputs are 1. A & B $\rightarrow$ Y 0 & 0 = 0, 1 & 1 = 1
**OR** Output is 1 if **ANY** input is 1. A + B $\rightarrow$ Y 0 + 0 = 0, 1 + 0 = 1
**NOT** Inverts the input. $\bar{A} \rightarrow$ Y NOT 1 = 0, NOT 0 = 1
