ADAPTATION OF LEAF TO PHOTOSYNTHESIS
  • The numerous stomata at the lower epidermis provide the passage for CARBON DIOXIDE and oxygen in to and out of the leaf respectively during photosynthesis

  • The xylem vessels conduct water to the leaves

  • The phloem vessels conduct, manufactured food (glucose) from the leaf to other part of the plant giving room for more storage of manufactured food

  • The pallisade cells, which are cylindrical and arranged at right angle close to each other maximize the absorbtion of sunlight

  • The transparent cuticle permit light to pass to the mesophyll cells

  • The pallisade cells in the mesophyll contain more, well positioned chloroplast to receive light

  • The waxy cuticle on the top most part of the leaf protects and prevent desication due to excessive water lost

  • The thin nature of leaves provide a short route for easy diffusion of carbon dioxide

  • The leaves have brought flat surface area to absorb carbon dioxide and sunlight

  • The large extra-cellular air space in the spongy mesophyll layer gives route for the easy circulation of gases
