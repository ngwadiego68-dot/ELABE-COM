ENDOTHERMIC AND EXOTHERMIC REACTION
  1. EXOTHERMIC REACTION
    2.

    An exothermic reaction is a reaction in which is given off to the surrounding.In this case, the energy of reactants is greater than that of product or energy liberated during bond formation is greater than that the energy absorbed durin bond breaking. hence heat change is negative(-).Example of exothermic reactions are

    CaO + H2O →Ca(OH)2
    CO2 + H2O→H2CO3

  2. ENDOTHERMIC REACTION
    3.

    An endothermic reaction is a reaction that release heat to tha environment. In this case the energy of products is greater than the energy of reactants or energy absorbed during bond breaking is greater than that needed for bond formation hence heat change is positive(+) . Example of an endothermic reaction is


    CaCO3(s)→CaO(s) + CO2(g)
