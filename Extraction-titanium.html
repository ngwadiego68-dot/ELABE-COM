23.4 The Extraction of Titanium (Ti)

Physical Properties and Occurrence

Properties of Titanium

  • It is a **hard** metal with a high melting point.
  • It resembles **stainless steel**.
  • It is famously **resistant to corrosion** and is generally considered inert.

Occurrence

Titanium is a transition series metal that is found in the combined state as two primary ores in Cameroon: **Rutile** (Titanium(IV) oxide, $\text{TiO}_2$) in **Akonolinga**, and **Ilmenite** ($\text{FeTiO}_3$).

The Extraction Process (Kroll Process)

Titanium is extracted from its ores using the **Kroll process**, which is primarily a **reduction process**. It involves three main stages:

  1. **Preparation** of titanium(IV) chloride ($\text{TiCl}_4$).
  2. **Purification** of titanium(IV) chloride.
  3. **Reduction** of titanium(IV) chloride.

Stage 1: Preparation of Titanium(IV) Chloride ($\text{TiCl}_4$)

The ore ($\text{TiO}_2$ or $\text{FeTiO}_3$) is mixed with **carbon** and heated in a stream of **chlorine** to produce the volatile titanium(IV) chloride.

$$\text{TiO}_{2(s)} + \text{C}_{(s)} + 2\text{Cl}_{2(g)} \rightarrow \text{TiCl}_{4(g)} + \text{CO}_{2(g)}$$ $$2\text{FeTiO}_{3(s)} + 3\text{C}_{(s)} + 7\text{Cl}_{2(g)} \rightarrow 2\text{TiCl}_{4(g)} + 2\text{FeCl}_{3(g)} + 3\text{CO}_{2(g)}$$

Stage 2 & 3: Purification and Reduction

The $\text{TiCl}_4$ produced is purified by **fractional distillation**. It is then reduced using a more reactive metal like **sodium** ($\text{Na}$) or **magnesium** ($\text{Mg}$) in an inert atmosphere.

An **atmosphere of argon** is necessary because titanium readily reacts with oxygen and nitrogen at elevated temperatures, which would contaminate the pure metal.

Reduction with Sodium:

$$\text{TiCl}_{4(g)} + 4\text{Na}_{(s)} \rightarrow \text{Ti}_{(s)} + 4\text{NaCl}_{(l)}$$

Reduction with Magnesium:

$$\text{TiCl}_{4(g)} + 2\text{Mg}_{(s)} \rightarrow \text{Ti}_{(s)} + 2\text{MgCl}_{2(s)}$$

Uses and Importance of Titanium

Titanium is extremely useful, especially for high-performance applications:

  • **Construction:** Used extensively for **steam turbines** and **chemical plants**.
  • **Aerospace:** Used in the construction of **aircrafts** and **space vehicles** due to its low density and high strength.
  • **Energy:** Used in **nuclear reactors**.
