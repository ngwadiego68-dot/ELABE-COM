INDUSTRIAL MANUFACTURE OF SULPHURIC ACID

H2SO4 is a compound of sulphur.It is manufactured industrially by the contact process.It Takes place in 4 stages namely:

  • preparation of SO2
  • purification of SO2
  • catalytic oxidation of SO2 to SO3(contact process
  • conversion of S03 to H2SO4
    •
The raw materials are sulphur from frash process and oxygen from fractional distillation of liauid air
process
  • sulphur burns in oxygen to produce SO2
    • S(g)+O2(g)→SO2(g)
  • The SO2 is mixed with excess air and the mixture is purified by passing it through a dust precipitator to remove dust particles that poison the catalyst and otheer impurities like Arsenic compounds which which can also poison the catalyst

  • The mixture is dried by passing it through concentrated H2SO4.It is then passed over finely divided vanadium pentaoxide(V2O5) at a temperature of 450°C to 500°C and pressure of 1atm to form sulphurtrioxide

    • SO2(g) + O2(g) →2S03(g)

  • The SO3 is cold and dissolved in already existing H2SO4 to form oleum(fuming H2SO4)

    • SO3 + H2SO4 → H2S2O7

  • After the reaction,the oleum is dissolved in calculated amout of water to form 98% pure H2SO4
    • H2S2O7 + 2H2O→ 2H2SO4

NOTE I:The SO3 IS FIRST DISSOLVED IN OLEUM BECAUSE(the reaction is exothermic when dissolved directly in water hence release alot of heat to the environment)

NOTE II:PLATINUM is the best catalyst for this prcess but it is not use for the following reasons;
  • It is very expensive.
  • It is easily poisoined by impurities.
